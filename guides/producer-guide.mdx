## Overview

As a **Producer**, you are responsible for managing inventory, tracking production batches, maintaining raw material stock, and ensuring products are ready for delivery. Your role focuses on the production and inventory aspects of the Fluxora system.

<Tip>
Producers have full access to inventory management features but limited access to other system areas.
</Tip>

## Key Responsibilities

Producers manage:
- Product inventory and production batches
- Raw materials and ingredient tracking
- Recipes and production formulas
- Stock level monitoring
- Production planning based on delivery schedules

## Product Management

### Viewing Products

Access the complete product catalog showing:
- Product name and type
- Current total stock across all batches
- Active batch count
- Production status

### Creating New Products

When introducing a new product:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Products">
    Go to **Inventory** > **Products**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Product">
    Click "New Product" and enter:
    - Product name (e.g., "Pan Corriente", "Pan Especial")
    - Product description
    - Unit of measure (typically kg for bread)
    - Category or type
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Initial Batch">
    After creating the product, create the first production batch with initial stock.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Updating Product Information

1. Locate the product in the product list
2. Click on the product to view details
3. Select "Edit"
4. Modify product information
5. Save changes

<Tip>
Products themselves don't have stock - stock is tracked at the batch (lote) level to enable proper FIFO (First In, First Out) inventory management.
</Tip>

## Production Batch Management

### Understanding Batches (Lotes)

Each production run creates a batch with:
- **Fecha de Producción**: Production date
- **Fecha de Vencimiento**: Expiration date
- **Cantidad Inicial**: Initial quantity produced
- **Stock Disponible**: Current available stock
- **Estado**: Status (available, expired, depleted)

### Creating Production Batches

<Card title="Creating a New Batch" icon="plus">
  Batches represent individual production runs and enable tracking of product freshness and FIFO inventory rotation.
</Card>

**Steps to create a batch:**

```bash
Endpoint: POST /api/inventario/productos/{productoId}/lotes
Permission: ADMIN, PRODUCER
```

1. Navigate to **Products** section
2. Select the product to produce
3. Click "New Batch" or "Produce"
4. Enter batch details:
   - Production date (defaults to today)
   - Expiration date (based on product shelf life)
   - Quantity produced (in kg)
5. Save the batch

### Viewing Batch Details

For each product, view:
- All active batches
- Batches sorted by expiration date
- Available stock per batch
- Batch production history

### Updating Batches

Modify batch information when needed:

```bash
Endpoint: PUT /api/inventario/productos/{productoId}/lotes/{loteId}
Permission: ADMIN, PRODUCER
```

- Correct production dates
- Adjust quantities (for count corrections)
- Update expiration dates
- Mark batches for priority use

### Stock Deduction

When deliveries are made, stock is automatically deducted:
- System uses FIFO (oldest batches first)
- Ensures freshest products reach customers
- Tracks which batches were used for each delivery

### Viewing Available Stock

Check current available stock:

```bash
Endpoint: GET /api/inventario/productos/{productoId}/lotes/disponibles
```

Shows only batches with available quantity that haven't expired.

## Raw Materials Management

### Viewing Raw Material Inventory

Access the raw materials (materias primas) section to see:

```bash
Endpoint: GET /api/inventario/materias-primas
Permission: ADMIN, PRODUCER
```

- Material name (flour, yeast, salt, etc.)
- Current stock quantity
- Unit of measure
- Cost per unit
- Supplier information

### Adding New Raw Materials

When tracking a new ingredient:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Raw Materials">
    Navigate to **Inventory** > **Raw Materials**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Material">
    Click "Add Material" and enter:
    - Material name
    - Unit of measure (kg, units, liters, etc.)
    - Initial stock quantity
    - Cost per unit
    - Supplier details
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Reorder Level">
    Optionally set a minimum stock level for alerts
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Recording Purchases

When raw materials arrive:

1. Go to **Raw Materials** > **Record Purchase**
2. Select the material
3. Enter:
   - Quantity received
   - Purchase cost
   - Purchase date
   - Supplier
4. System automatically updates stock levels

### Updating Stock Levels

Manually adjust stock for corrections:

```bash
Endpoint: PATCH /api/inventario/materias-primas/{id}/stock?cantidad={cantidad}
```

- Positive values add to stock
- Negative values reduce stock
- Use for inventory counts or corrections

### Low Stock Alerts

<Warning>
Monitor raw materials regularly to avoid production delays. Set reorder points to receive alerts before materials run out.
</Warning>

## Recipe Management

### Understanding Recipes

Recipes (recetas) define:
- Which raw materials are needed
- Quantity of each material
- Material costs
- Production yield

This enables:
- Accurate cost calculation
- Production planning
- Material requirement forecasting

### Creating Recipes

<Card title="Recipe Creation" icon="book">
  Each product should have at least one recipe defining its ingredients and proportions.
</Card>

**Steps to create a recipe:**

1. Navigate to **Recipes** section
2. Click "New Recipe"
3. Select the product this recipe is for
4. Add ingredients:
   - Select raw material
   - Enter quantity needed per unit of production
   - Repeat for all ingredients
5. Save the recipe

### Viewing Product Recipes

For any product, view:

```bash
Endpoint: GET /api/inventario/recetas/producto/{productoId}
```

- All associated recipes
- Ingredient lists
- Quantities and proportions
- Total cost per production unit

### Updating Recipes

When formulas change:

1. Locate the recipe
2. Select "Edit"
3. Modify ingredients or quantities
4. Save changes

<Tip>
Keep historical recipes even when updating. This helps track cost changes over time and maintains production records.
</Tip>

### Deleting Recipes

Remove obsolete recipes:
- Verify recipe is no longer in use
- Confirm deletion
- System removes recipe and all ingredient associations

## Inventory Tracking

### Stock Verification

Regularly verify stock accuracy:

<Accordion title="Daily Checks">
  - Review available stock by product
  - Check batch expiration dates
  - Identify products nearing low stock
  - Verify production completed successfully
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Weekly Audits">
  - Physical inventory count
  - Compare system vs actual stock
  - Adjust discrepancies
  - Review slow-moving products
</Accordion>

### Waste Tracking (Mermas)

Record production waste and losses:
1. Navigate to **Waste Management**
2. Select product and batch
3. Enter:
   - Quantity lost
   - Reason (burnt, damaged, quality issue, etc.)
   - Date
4. System adjusts stock levels

### Batch-Level Queries

Use batch endpoints for detailed tracking:

```bash
# Get specific batch details
GET /api/inventario/productos/lotes/{loteId}

# Get multiple batches at once
GET /api/inventario/productos/lotes-batch?ids=1,2,3
```

## Best Practices

### Daily Producer Workflow

1. **Early Morning**:
   - Review today's delivery schedule
   - Check production requirements
   - Verify raw material availability
   - Plan production batches

2. **During Production**:
   - Record batches as they complete
   - Enter accurate quantities
   - Mark expiration dates
   - Track any waste

3. **End of Day**:
   - Verify all batches recorded
   - Check stock levels
   - Plan tomorrow's needs
   - Identify reorder requirements

### Inventory Management Tips

<Tip>
**FIFO is Critical**: Always use oldest batches first to minimize waste and ensure product freshness. The system does this automatically during deliveries.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Buffer Stock**: Maintain 10-15% buffer stock above scheduled deliveries to handle unexpected orders or quality issues.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Expiration Tracking**: Monitor expiration dates daily. Plan promotions or special offers for products nearing expiration.
</Tip>

### Quality Control

- Verify batch quality before recording
- Mark defective batches separately
- Track quality issues for process improvement
- Maintain consistent batch sizes

### Data Accuracy

<Warning>
Accurate inventory data is critical. Always:
- Record production immediately
- Double-check quantities
- Update waste/losses promptly
- Reconcile physical counts regularly
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

<Accordion title="Stock shows negative">
  This indicates deliveries were recorded without sufficient batch stock. Check recent deliveries and create correction batches if needed.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Cannot create batch">
  Verify you have PRODUCER or ADMIN role. Ensure the product exists and all required fields are completed.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Raw material not updating">
  Check that purchases are being recorded correctly. Verify the material ID matches between purchase records and inventory.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Recipe costs seem wrong">
  Review raw material costs per unit. Update material prices if they've changed. Verify recipe quantities are correct.
</Accordion>

## Role Permissions

As a PRODUCER, you can:

✅ View and manage all products
✅ Create, update, and delete production batches
✅ Manage raw materials inventory
✅ Create and modify recipes
✅ View production plans and requirements
✅ Generate inventory reports

❌ Cannot manage users or system settings
❌ Cannot assign routes or manage deliveries
❌ Cannot access financial reports (admin only)
❌ Cannot manage clients

## Getting Help

If you encounter issues:

1. Verify your role permissions (PRODUCER)
2. Check that product/batch IDs are correct
3. Review error messages for specific guidance
4. Contact your administrator for permission issues
5. Document inventory discrepancies for review

<Tip>
Your role is crucial to the supply chain. Accurate inventory management ensures drivers have the products they need and customers receive fresh, quality goods.
</Tip>
