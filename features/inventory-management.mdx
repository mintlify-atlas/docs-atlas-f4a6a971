## Overview

Fluxora's Inventory Management system provides comprehensive tools for tracking raw materials, creating production recipes, managing production batches, and monitoring inventory levels in real-time. The system ensures you always have the materials needed for production while minimizing waste.

## Raw Materials

### Material Catalog

Raw materials (materias primas) are the basic ingredients used in your recipes. Each material includes:

- **ID**: Unique identifier (auto-generated)
- **Name** (nombre): Material name (e.g., "Flour", "Sugar", "Eggs")
- **Unit** (unidad): Measurement unit (kg, liters, units)

### Material Batches (Lotes)

Instead of tracking a single quantity, Fluxora uses a batch system where each purchase is recorded separately:

**LoteMateriaPrima Fields:**
- **Quantity**: Amount purchased in this batch
- **Cost**: Total cost of the batch
- **Unit Cost**: Calculated cost per unit
- **Purchase Date**: When the material was acquired
- **Expiration Date**: When the material expires (if applicable)
- **Status**: Available, depleted, or expired

### Managing Raw Materials

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Materials">
    Navigate to **Dashboard > Inventory > Raw Materials**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add New Material">
    Click **New Material** and enter:
    - Material name
    - Measurement unit (kg, L, units)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Record Purchases">
    When you purchase materials:
    1. Find the material in the catalog
    2. Click **Record Purchase**
    3. Enter quantity, cost, and purchase date
    4. Add expiration date if applicable
    5. Save the batch
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Track Usage">
    Materials are automatically consumed when you create production batches
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The batch system enables accurate cost tracking and FIFO (First In, First Out) inventory management.
</Note>

## Recipes

### Recipe Structure

Recipes define the ingredients needed to produce specific products. Fluxora supports two recipe types:

#### 1. Simple Recipes (Receta)

Basic ingredient list for a product:

- **Product ID** (productoId): Which product this recipe creates
- **Material ID** (materiaPrimaId): Which raw material is needed
- **Required Quantity** (cantidadNecesaria): How much of the material
- **Unit** (unidad): Measurement unit

#### 2. Master Recipes (RecetaMaestra)

Complex recipes with additional details:

- **Recipe name**: Descriptive name
- **Category**: Product category (panaderia, pasteleria, etc.)
- **Unit price**: Selling price per unit
- **Multiple ingredients**: List of all required materials

### Creating a Recipe

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Recipes">
    Go to **Dashboard > Inventory > Recipes**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Create Recipe">
    Click **New Recipe** button
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Recipe Details">
    - Recipe name (e.g., "White Bread - 1kg")
    - Product category
    - Selling price per unit
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Ingredients">
    For each ingredient:
    1. Select raw material from dropdown
    2. Enter required quantity
    3. Specify unit of measurement
    4. Click **Add Ingredient**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Recipe">
    Review all ingredients and click **Save Recipe**
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Production & Batches

### Products

Products (productos) are the finished goods you manufacture and sell:

**Product Fields:**
- **ID**: Unique identifier
- **Name** (nombre): Product name
- **Selling Price** (precioVenta): Price per unit
- **Product Type** (tipoProducto): CORRIENTE, ESPECIAL, or NO_APLICA
- **Category** (categoria): panaderia, pasteleria, etc.
- **Status** (estado): activo or descontinuado
- **Master Recipe ID** (recetaMaestraId): Associated recipe (optional)

### Production Batches (Lotes de Producto)

When you produce items, you create production batches (LoteProducto):

**Batch Fields:**
- **Product ID** (productoId): Which product was produced
- **Quantity Produced** (cantidadProducida): Total units made
- **Current Stock** (stockActual): Remaining inventory
- **Total Production Cost** (costoProduccionTotal): Sum of material costs
- **Unit Cost** (costoUnitario): Cost per unit produced
- **Production Date** (fechaProduccion): When batch was made
- **Expiration Date** (fechaVencimiento): When batch expires
- **Status** (estado): disponible, agotado, or vencido

### Creating a Production Batch

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Production">
    Navigate to **Dashboard > Inventory > Production**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Product">
    Choose which product you're producing from the dropdown
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Batch Details">
    - Quantity to produce (e.g., 100 kg)
    - Production date (default: today)
    - Expiration date (if applicable)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Material Requirements">
    The system calculates required materials based on the recipe:
    - Shows what materials are needed
    - Indicates if you have sufficient stock
    - Warns about material shortages
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Production">
    Click **Start Production**:
    - Materials are consumed from inventory
    - Production cost is calculated automatically
    - New batch is added to product inventory
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Cost Calculation

Fluxora automatically calculates production costs:

```
Total Production Cost = Sum of (Material Quantity × Material Unit Cost)
Unit Cost = Total Production Cost ÷ Quantity Produced
Unit Profit = Selling Price - Unit Cost
```

<Note>
  The system uses FIFO (First In, First Out) to consume materials, ensuring older batches are used first.
</Note>

## Inventory Tracking

### Stock Levels

The Inventory Dashboard displays real-time stock levels for:

- **Raw Materials**: Total available quantity across all batches
- **Products**: Total available quantity across all production batches
- **Low Stock Alerts**: Materials or products below threshold
- **Expiring Soon**: Items approaching expiration date

### Inventory Alerts

The system provides automatic notifications for:

- **Low Stock**: When material or product quantity falls below minimum
- **Expiring Materials**: Materials approaching expiration date (within 7 days)
- **Expired Batches**: Batches past expiration date
- **Out of Stock**: Products with zero inventory

## Waste & Loss Management

### Recording Product Loss (Mermas)

When products are damaged, expired, or otherwise lost, record them as mermas:

**MermaProducto Fields:**
- **Product ID**: Which product was lost
- **Batch ID**: Which production batch
- **Quantity Lost**: Amount of waste
- **Reason**: Why the loss occurred
- **Date**: When the loss was recorded

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Merma Modal">
    Go to **Dashboard > Inventory > Production > Merma** tab
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Product and Batch">
    Choose the affected product and specific batch
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Loss Details">
    - Quantity lost
    - Reason (expired, damaged, quality issue)
    - Additional notes
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Record">
    System updates:
    - Batch stock is reduced
    - Loss is recorded for reporting
    - Cost of loss is tracked
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Use Cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Starting a new production run">
    1. Go to **Inventory > Production**
    2. Review current stock levels
    3. Select product to produce
    4. Enter quantity needed (based on orders)
    5. System shows material requirements
    6. Confirm materials are available
    7. Click **Start Production**
    8. Enter production date and expiration
    9. Save batch - materials consumed, inventory updated
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Receiving a material shipment">
    1. Go to **Inventory > Raw Materials**
    2. Find the material in the catalog
    3. Click **Record Purchase**
    4. Enter:
       - Quantity received
       - Total cost paid
       - Purchase date
       - Supplier (in notes)
       - Expiration date
    5. Save - new batch added to inventory
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Handling expired products">
    1. Go to **Inventory > Production > Merma**
    2. Filter by expired batches
    3. For each expired batch:
       - Click **Register Loss**
       - Select "Expired" as reason
       - Enter quantity to discard
       - Save record
    4. Batch status changes to "vencido"
    5. Stock is reduced accordingly
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Calculating product profitability">
    1. Go to production batch details
    2. View **Unit Cost** (calculated from materials)
    3. Compare to **Selling Price**
    4. Calculate: Profit = Selling Price - Unit Cost
    5. Evaluate if pricing needs adjustment
    6. Consider:
       - Material cost changes
       - Production efficiency
       - Market competition
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Tips & Tricks

- **Recipe Scaling**: Create recipes for standard batch sizes (e.g., 10kg, 50kg) and scale as needed
- **Safety Stock**: Maintain buffer inventory of 10-20% for high-demand products
- **Material Ordering**: Order materials 3-5 days before needed to ensure timely delivery
- **Expiration Tracking**: Set system reminders for materials with short shelf life
- **Cost Monitoring**: Review production costs monthly to identify cost-saving opportunities
- **Seasonal Planning**: Adjust recipes and inventory for holiday and seasonal demand
